Common Stocks

Common Stock is a share of a company. Stock holders influence the company by electing on the board of directors and voting on corporate policies. Stocks are constantly being bought and sold on the stock market. Their price is determined by supply and demand.

Common Stocks are high risk. They are the most volatile of all investments, but they also tend to outperform other investments
Common Stocks do not have any interest. Stocks must be sold to earn return. Some stocks pay dividends.
Common Stocks are not insured. All of the risk of this investment is held by the investor.

Pros Cons
  • Common Stock yields large gains
  • Many different types of stock to choose from
  • Easy to buy and sell
  • Common Stock has high risks and high volatity
  • Lack of control over the sucess of investment

Common Stocks are suited for...


Younger to middle age

Younger people will have the best chances investing in common stock as they have time on their side to for their investments to appreciate, or to recover from bad investments.

Mid to high income

Stocks are high risk invesments. That being said, investors need to have the income to afford the volatile nature of the stock market. They must have the finances to purchase and recover from bad investments.

People looking for high risk and high return

Stocks are where you want to be if you want to make profit. Stocks by far have the greatest return in comparison to any other investments.
