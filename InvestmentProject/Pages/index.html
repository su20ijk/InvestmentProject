The Basics to Investing


Start Young

The best investors don't need a lot of money (although that does help): they need time. Starting young is essential for a number of reasons. You can take more risks when in your youth, as you have time to recover from any poor ones. You will develop better spending habitats throughout your life time by watching how you use your money. But most importantly, being young maximizes the effectiveness of compound interests. With each return you are making more money. The longer you have that money in your savings the more money you will accrue. Eventually in the long run, you make more with less money than you would if you start late.

Risk & Return

The key concept to investing is Risk & Return: the relationship of the amount of revenue gained and amount of risk taken on any investment. Investments with lower return rates make up for it by being stable for the long term. By being safe, these investments have smaller returns than others. More risky investments, however, have to entice investors with higher return rates. Due to the higher chance of underperformance or loss of capital, these investments have to have larger returns. Therefore, the higher the risk, the higher the return. The lower the risk, the lower the return.

Diversify

Make sure to diversify your portfolio! It's very important to invest into many different types of investments, as every investment has its own risks and rewards. The events that could make one investment successful may be detrimental to another. By having multiple varying investments, no one event should negatively affect all your assets. Diversification minimizes the volatility and risks of investments while keeping capital gain steady. That being said, make sure to invest in only what you understand and are comfortable with. In short, diversifying your portfolio will better balance the risk and return of your assets and investments.

October 1929

×

When homes and autos selling were doing extraordinary in the 1920s, investors started borrowing more money than they could afford to purchase more stocks. And when the market went done, those investors can't pay for the huge amount of debt, and slid into bankruptcy. On the fourth day of the crash, the stock market fell 12.92%(also known as "Black Monday"). It lead to the Great Depression, and the stock market took 12 years to recover.

October 19, 1987

×

As technology began to play a role in the stock market, people started to trade more and more frequently. Companies started leveraging questionable financing tools, share prices dropped heavily. On October 19, 1987; as more investors sold, more investors panicked and sold aggressively. This lead to a a 23% lose of the stock market's value in one day, broke the record of the highest stock market drop in one day. It took another 12 years for the stock market to recover, and it is also known as "Black Monday the 2nd".

1999-2000

×

Technology is again front and center. In the 1990s, "new economy" companies like AOL, Pets.com saw their share prices rise substantially. Only two years later, those dot.com companies fell out of favor as investors fled highly inflated tech stocks. Two years after that, their stock price went from $87 to under $1 per share. Some of them like Globe.com and Nasdaq fell from 50000 to just 1000 in one year. This was recovered after Wall Street began to rate those tech companies more accurately, and investors think more and be more comservative when they purchase stocks and funds.

2008

×

In 2008, mortgage-backed securities got widespread. Those high-rating mortgage-backed security contains some really risky investments in housing. Since it was doing too well, house price started increasing constantly at a high rate. More and more people couldn't afford their home mortgages, so they stopped paying. This caused housing price to drop dramatically, and crashed the economy and the stock market since the "too big to fail" banks and companies were also bankrupted. This was recovered after the U.S.congress approved a massiv egovernment funding that secures and stabilizes the market. This is also known as "The Great Recession".

Super Bowl Index

A super bowl win for a team from the NFL foretells a decline in the stock market the coming year.

Bear Market

The stock market being in a downward trend or a period of falling stock prices(or expected to) for several months or several years.

Bull Market

The stock market being in a upward trend or a period of rising stock prices(or expected to) for several months or several years.

Blue Chip Stocks

The stocks behind large, industry-leading companies.

Pink Sheet Stocks

Refers to penny stocks, which are traded at $5 per share or less.

Volatility

The price movements of a stock or the stock market as a whole.

Bid-Ask Spread

The difference between what peple have to spend and what people want to get.

Beta

A measurement of the relationship between the price of a stock and the movement of the whole market.

Forex

Stands for foreign exchange, involves trading different curencies.
