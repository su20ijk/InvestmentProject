Corporate Bonds

Corporate Bonds are a type of debt security sold by companies to investors. This is simply money being loaned to and paid back by a corporation. Corporate Bonds are in denominations of $1000s, with the least expensive ones being $5000 and $10000.

Corporate Bonds are higher risk investments. These bonds are only backed by the corporation and their credit quality.
Corporate Bonds can have fixed, floating, and step coupon rates. Both APY and maturities depend on the coupon rate.
Corporate Bonds are not insured. The FDIC does not cover corporate bonds. Insurance can be bought for the issuer.

Pros Cons
  • Lots of variety in the market to choose from
  • Many options for coupon structures
  • Better yield compared to government bonds
  • Corporate bonds are liquid: they can traded
  • Higher risk than government bonds as corporations can have varying credit ratings
