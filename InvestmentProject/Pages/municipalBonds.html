Municipal Bonds

Municipal Bonds are bonds issued by state and local governments to finance anything from highways, state buildings, and public works to sport arenas and civic improvements. Typical municpal bonds have a minimum denomination of $5,000, but there are some issues with a minimum denomination of $100,000. Municipal bonds are tax-exempt from federal income tax.

Munis are low risk because the states and cities have the power to tax; their governments can pay any interest and principal.
Munis have low interest rates. Governments issue lower interest rates in exchange for the investments being tax-exempt.
Munis can be insured, not by the FDIC or the government, but by private insurance companies.

Pros Cons
  • Munis are tax-exempt from the federal, and possibly state and local income tax
  • Munis are safe investments, as they are less volatile, and issued by the government
  • Munis do not compete against inflation rates as well as other investments

Municpal Bonds are suited for...


Any Age

Municipal Bonds like any bond are suited for all ages. Time of course is a huge factor, so younger investors tend to benefit. However, municipal bonds also provide steady income for retirees.

Middle to High income

Municipal Bonds sell with the lowest being around $5000. Therefore, investors should have the resources to expend on this investment, as the investment itself cannot compete against inflation rates

Investors looking for steady tax-free income

The most notable feature of Muncipal Bonds is that they are tax-free on the federal, and sometimes state and local levels. If an investor wants to lower their taxes and have low-risk investments, Municipal Bonds are a good option
