Federal Government Bonds are bonds issued by the federal government and sold by the U.S. Treasury Department. These bonds are used for the government to increase capital, spend on infrastructure, and control the money supply.

CDs are low risk as they are FDIC insured, and most penalities are within control of the depositor.
CDs have fixed interest rates and fixed maturities. CD rates are quoted as annual percentage yields (APY).
CDs are covered by FDIC insurance, as long as its value does not exceed the $250,000 limit.

Pros Cons
  • CDs under $250,000 are FDIC insured
  • CDs have higher interest rates than regular savings accounts
  • Compound interest increases total CD return
  • CDs are not liquid: there are eary withdrawal penalites
  • CD interest rates do not keep up with the rate of inflation
