Federal Government Bonds

Federal Government Bonds are bonds issued by federal governments. In the United States, the issued bonds are sold by the U.S. Treasury Department. These bonds are used for the government to increase capital, spend on infrastructure, and control the money supply. The minimum denomination is $1000 and maturity can be between 10 to 30 years.

Federal Government bonds are low risk as the government has the power to tax to pay interest. Our government has never made failed to pay back a loan.
Federal Government bonds have fixed interest rates and fixed maturities. Coupon payments are made semianually.
Federal Government bonds are not covered by FDIC insurance, but they are backed in full faith and credit of the government.

Pros Cons
  • Generally safe with low volatility and default risk
  • Government tend to pay steady income
  • Easy to buy and sell on the market
  • Lower rates of return compared to corporate bonds
  • Interest rates do not keep up with the rate of inflation
  • Government bonds in emerging markets may carry more risk

Federal Government Bonds are suited for...


Any

Like all bonds, Federal Government Bonds have maturities. The principle will not be paid until the end of the term. Most of these bonds can have maturities from 10 to 30 years, so the investor better have time on his side.

On the other hand, these bonds can be a steady source of income for those in or nearing retirement, making them benifical to older folks.

Any income

Federal Government Bonds are relatively affordable, with the minimum denomination being $1000. However, these interest rates do not keep up with inflation, so investors should have other sources of income.

Investors looking for steady returns

Federal Government Bonds are backed by the US government. The government has never made a late payment, nor has it ever defaulted on a loan. If safety is important to the investor, than this is the investment they want
