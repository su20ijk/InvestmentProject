Preferred Stocks

Preferred Stock are shares of a company that have higher claim to dividends. Unlike common stock, however, preferred stockholders have little to no voting power within the company. They tend not to have maturities. In a way, preferred stocks are a middle ground to stocks and bonds.

CDs are low risk as they are FDIC insured, and most penalities are within control of the depositor.
Preferred Stock have fixed or benchmark set interest rates . They can be paid semianually or monthly
Preferred Stock is not FDIC insured. All risk is held by the investor.

Pros Cons
  • Preferred Stock has larger yield than bonds
  • Preferred Stock are taxes as capital gains, not income
  • Preferred Stock holders have priority ahead of common stock holders when paying dividends
  • Preferred Stock is callable, meaning the issuer can buy it back
  • Company growth has little to no affect on preferred stock

Preferred Stocks are suited for...


Any age

Preferred Stocks are suitable for any age as they do not have maturities, nor do they appreciate based on company growth. Time is not a major factor in preferred stock value, therefore any age can reap the full benefits of the investment.

Any income

As long as an investor can afford preferred stock, they are suited to invest in. Preferred Stock provides a decent and steady return, so in the long run, it should pay off.

Investors looking for steady returns

For the most part, Preferred stock provides a steady return. As long as the investment is sound, investors should receive their dividends from the company.
